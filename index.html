Why plants change
how a room feels.

Biophilic design isn't decoration — it measurably changes how people work, rest, and feel in a space. The science is well-documented; the difference is hard to miss.

87%

Cleaner Air

Many indoor plants measurably reduce common airborne toxins, improving air quality in the spaces where people spend most of their time.

38%

Higher Productivity

Offices with thoughtful plant design see meaningful gains in focus and creative output across teams.

15%

Less Stress

Greenery is linked to lower stress hormones and improved mood — quietly, every day, in the background.

25%

Sharper Focus

Biophilic environments support concentration, memory, and overall cognitive well-being.

How we work.

Four steps from first conversation to thriving installation — with optional monthly maintenance after.

01

Free Consultation

We walk your space, talk through your vision, look at lighting and conditions, and discuss budget honestly. No pressure, no obligation.

02

Custom Design

A plant plan tailored to your space: species selected for your light and climate, containers chosen for your aesthetic, layouts that make the room work harder.

03

Professional Install

We handle delivery, placement, and setup. You see your space transformed in a day — without lifting a planter.

04

Ongoing Care (optional)

Monthly maintenance: watering, pruning, health checks, seasonal rotations, and a replacement guarantee. Your plants thrive, you stay focused on your work.

About Tropicalogy

Meet Nathan.

"Tropicalogy is the study of all things tropical — plants and vibes. I started this to do plant design the way I'd want it done for my own space: personal, well-considered, and a little bit unexpected."

Nathan founded Tropicalogy to bring genuinely thoughtful plant design to Tampa Bay — moving past the same tired species and containers, and treating each space as its own ecosystem. Whether it's a single desk plant or a full atrium, the bar is the same: pick what thrives, style it well, and care for it properly.
